Breast cancer tips
Cancer on the rise beware

Breast Cancer in sub Saharan Africa is on the rise though it is not as common as it is in developed countries The reasons for this scenario are many; they include low awareness, late stage with which women come to hospital, failure to access care.

Embrace regular checks

Breast Cancer, especially in early stages causes no pain, it flies below radar. Absence of pain is construed as being well, absence of pain does not necessarily mean all is well and the presence of pain does not necessarily mean doom either.

How and who checks

The safest way to play is to opt for regular checks at three levels:
Level 1 - self-check,
Level 2 - Be checked by a trained practitioner,
Level 3 - Use Imaging as recommended by a health worker, these include a breast ultrasound scan or a mammogram.
